THE FOUNDER

Pedro Evangelista Llamas was born in Pagsanjan, Laguna on August 01, 1884. He studied at St. Joseph Catholic School in Hongkong and later earned his Bachelor of Science in Pharmaceutical Chemistry from the University of Michigan.

He founded Pagsanjan Academy in 1923 and later established Laguna Institute in 1930 to address the need for secondary education in Calamba.

Despite the challenges of World War II, Laguna Institute continued its mission. Don Pedro Llamas passed away on January 13, 1946, but his legacy of quality education continues through LCBA.

PHILOSOPHY

Inspired by Dr. Jose Rizal’s educational principles, LCBA upholds a balanced curriculum towards holistic development for a better quality of life.

VISION

LCBA envisions becoming an autonomous higher education institution committed to producing holistically developed and globally competitive lifelong learners.

MISSION

LCBA commits itself to total human development through value-laden education, policy-relevant research, and sustainable community involvement.
